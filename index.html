A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainuladeen Abdul Kalam Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was a distinguished aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam rose from humble beginnings through his dedication to education and science. He played a crucial role in India's space and missile programs, particularly the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. His contributions at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) were pivotal to India's defense capabilities.As President from 2002 to 2007, Kalam was loved for his humility and vision for India's future. He authored "India 2020," outlining a plan to transform India into a developed nation. Known as the "People's President," he remained approachable and dedicated to inspiring the youth. After his presidency, he continued to engage with students, giving lectures and writing books to motivate young minds. His belief in dreaming big and the power of education to drive change left a profound legacy.
